Building Owners and Accountable Persons: Fire Door Responsibility
At Firechecked.co.uk, we specialize in providing comprehensive fire door surveys to help building owners and accountable persons meet their legal obligations. Below is a simplified outline of what building owners must do according to UK law, focusing on the importance of regular fire door surveys.
Conduct Regular Fire Door Surveys

Legal Requirement: Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 and the Fire Safety Act 2021, building owners are legally required to ensure that all fire doors are fit for purpose and properly maintained.

Actions Required:
  • Schedule Regular Surveys: Arrange for professional fire door surveys at least every six months, or more frequently if specified by legislation or risk assessments.
  • Use Qualified Professionals: Engage certified fire door inspectors, like those at Firechecked.co.uk, who are knowledgeable about current regulations and standards.
The Assessment Components

Compliance Standards: Fire doors must meet standards set out in BS 9999:2017 and BS 8214:2016.

Actions Required:
  • Check for Damage: Identify any signs of damage, wear, or tampering that could compromise the door’s effectiveness.
  • Assess Components: Ensure that seals, hinges, handles, glazing, and self-closing devices are intact and functioning properly.
  • Certification Labels: Verify that fire doors have the appropriate certification labels or markings.
Maintain Detailed Records

Documentation: Keeping accurate records is crucial for demonstrating compliance and can be critical in the event of an inspection by authorities.

Actions Required:
  • Record Survey Findings: Document all findings from fire door surveys, including any defects noted and remedial actions taken.
  • Maintain a Logbook: Use a dedicated fire safety logbook or digital record to track inspections, maintenance, and repairs.
Promptly Address Identified Issues

Legal Responsibility: Failure to rectify known deficiencies can lead to legal penalties, including fines.

Actions Required:
  • Implement Repairs: Carry out necessary repairs as recommended in the fire door survey report.
  • Replace if Necessary: Replace fire doors that are beyond repair to ensure compliance.
  • Follow Professional Guidance: Utilize the expertise of fire door specialists to ensure repairs are conducted correctly
Educate Occupants and Staff

Awareness: Building occupants should understand the importance of fire doors and not interfere with their operation.

Actions Required:
  • Provide Information: Share guidance on fire door safety with tenants, employees, and visitors.
  • Training: Offer training sessions for staff on fire safety procedures and how to report issues.
  • Signage: Install clear signage indicating fire doors and instructions such as “Fire Door Keep Shut.”
